## Given — chakrit's standing rulings (inputs to the walk, not walk output)

Stated by chakrit at the srv review, before the walk. Commandments: they bind until he
explicitly releases them, and the walk works *within* them. Canonical home is here — the
resume links, never re-states, so the two can't drift.

(The prior record's "the original rulings are superseded by the record where they differ"
is exactly backwards — dead line, do not honor it.)

1. **No `/api` route prefix on the backend** — that's a frontend convention; backend
   routes are bare (`/health`, not `/api/health`).
2. **Fewer, bigger controller actions** that nudge things into the correct state — not a
   small action per little thing (convergent/idempotent over granular CRUD verbs).
3. **RESTful auth surface, his exact set:** `GET /health` · `GET /session` (current
   session) · `GET /users/me` (own profile) · `DELETE /session` (logout) ·
   `GET /auth/github` + `GET /auth/github/callback` · `POST /hooks/github` · `GET /setup`.
4. **Migrations never auto-run at boot** — run manually or triggered from a "systems" mode
   dashboard. (Current `Serve` auto-migrates: change owed.)
5. **No requeue-orphans boot action** — a worker launches pending builds at a set
   interval; boot does nothing special beyond starting that job.
6. **These prefs translate everywhere, including API design** — check whether
   `general-coding` already encodes them; likely route to the school (`general-coding`
   and/or `prod9-fx`) as durable conventions once the walk settles.
